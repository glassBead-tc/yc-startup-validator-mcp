# Garry Tan Startup Evaluation Framework

## Core Philosophy

Garry Tan's evaluation approach centers on **contrarian thinking**, **market timing**, and **founder-market fit**. He looks for startups that emerge when multiple factors converge to create unique opportunities, built by founders who have discovered insights that others have missed. His framework emphasizes nonobvious markets, fringe technologies, and the "why now" moment that makes success inevitable.

**Central Question:** "What have you figured out that nobody else has?"

## The "Why Now?" Framework

### The Convergence Theory
Garry believes the most powerful startups emerge when three factors intersect:

1. **New technologies** that enable novel solutions
2. **Changes in human behavior** that create new needs  
3. **Shifts in regulations** that open opportunities or demand compliance

### The "Why Now?" Test
- **Core Question**: Why couldn't this work five years ago, but will crush it today?
- **Timing Indicators**: Market forces that make now the perfect moment
- **Urgency Factor**: What makes this opportunity time-sensitive?

### Examples of "Why Now" Moments:
- **Smartphones enabling on-demand services** (Uber, Instacart)
- **GDPR creating urgency for privacy compliance** solutions
- **Remote work shifts** creating new collaboration needs
- **AI capabilities** reaching practical thresholds

## Founder-Market Fit: The "Earned Secrets" Model

### Definition
Founder-market fit means founders have **"earned secrets"** through direct experience that make them uniquely suited to solve their chosen problem.

### Key Evaluation Criteria:

#### 1. Deep Industry Understanding
- **Insider knowledge** of how the industry actually works
- **Firsthand experience** with the pain points being solved
- **Understanding of industry dynamics** others miss

#### 2. Unique Positioning
- **Access to distribution channels** others don't have
- **Relationships and networks** that provide advantages
- **Technical or domain expertise** that creates barriers

#### 3. Authentic Problem Recognition
- **Personal experience** with the problem
- **Witnessing the problem** in their professional context
- **Understanding why** existing solutions fail

### The Founder-Market Fit Spectrum:
- **High fit**: Founders who lived the problem and understand the solution deeply
- **Medium fit**: Founders who can learn and adapt quickly with strong execution
- **Low fit**: Founders attracted to market opportunity without deep understanding

## The "Hair on Fire" vs "Done Right" Dichotomy

### Two Paths to Success:

#### Path 1: Hair on Fire Problems
- **Definition**: Desperate problems that have never been solved before
- **Characteristics**: Users actively seeking solutions, high willingness to pay
- **Evaluation**: How bad is the problem? Who has it? How have you solved it?
- **Challenge**: Getting people to know you exist in new categories

#### Path 2: Done Right Solutions  
- **Definition**: Existing problems solved dramatically better
- **Characteristics**: Crowded markets but broken solutions
- **Evaluation**: How is your solution uniquely different and better?
- **Challenge**: Clear differentiation from existing solutions

### The "Done Right" Test:
- **Question**: "What have you figured out that nobody else has?"
- **Evidence**: Specific insights about why existing solutions fail
- **Differentiation**: How the solution is not just incrementally better

## The Revenue Reality Check

### "Edible" Revenue Focus
Garry emphasizes **"edible" revenue** - money that can actually pay salaries, buy groceries, and pay bills.

### Revenue Quality Evaluation:
- **Real vs. Projected**: Actual revenue vs. bookings or projections
- **Gross Profit Focus**: Revenue minus direct costs
- **Sustainability**: Revenue that can support business operations
- **Growth Trajectory**: Week-over-week and month-over-month growth

### Red Flags:
- **"Top line" or "bookings"** instead of net revenue
- **Future commitments** presented as current revenue
- **Vanity metrics** without underlying business fundamentals

## The Nonobvious Market Advantage

### Contrarian Market Thesis
Garry looks for **nonobvious markets** that others dismiss but have hidden potential.

### Market Evaluation Framework:

#### 1. Market Timing Assessment
- **Too early**: Technology or behavior not ready
- **Just right**: Perfect convergence of factors
- **Too late**: Market already saturated

#### 2. Hidden Market Signals
- **Fringe adoption**: Early adopters using imperfect solutions
- **Behavior changes**: People doing things differently
- **Technology enablers**: New capabilities reaching practical thresholds

#### 3. Conventional Wisdom Challenges
- **What everyone "knows"** about the market
- **Why that conventional wisdom** might be wrong
- **What the founder sees** that others miss

## The Earnestness Factor

### The #1 Characteristic
**Earnestness** - incredible sincerity and authenticity in the founder's approach.

### Earnestness Indicators:
- **Genuine passion** for solving the problem
- **Authentic motivation** beyond just making money
- **Deep conviction** about the solution
- **Willingness to do unglamorous work** to make it happen

### Warning Signs:
- **Opportunistic thinking** - jumping on trends without conviction
- **Shallow understanding** of the problem space
- **Focus on fundraising** rather than building

## The Growth Metrics Framework

### Key Performance Indicators:

#### 1. Week-over-Week Growth
- **Primary metric**: Consistent weekly growth rate
- **Compounding effect**: Small weekly gains that multiply
- **Momentum indicator**: Whether growth is accelerating or slowing

#### 2. User Engagement Depth
- **Quality over quantity**: Better to have fewer engaged users than many passive ones
- **Retention metrics**: How many users come back and use regularly
- **Usage intensity**: How deeply users engage with the product

#### 3. Market Expansion Signals
- **Word-of-mouth growth**: Users telling others organically
- **Market penetration**: Expanding within target segments
- **New use cases**: Users finding unexpected applications

## Red Flags in Garry's Framework

### Founder Red Flags:
1. **Lack of earnestness** - seems opportunistic or insincere
2. **Poor founder-market fit** - no deep understanding of the problem
3. **Inability to articulate "why now"** - can't explain timing advantage
4. **No unique insights** - can't answer "what have you figured out?"

### Market Red Flags:
1. **Obvious markets** - everyone sees the same opportunity
2. **No timing advantage** - could have been built anytime
3. **Crowded space without differentiation** - "me too" solutions
4. **Fake urgency** - manufactured "why now" story

### Business Red Flags:
1. **Vanity revenue** - bookings or projections instead of real money
2. **No path to profitability** - can't explain unit economics
3. **Unsustainable growth** - growth that requires constant capital
4. **Lack of defensibility** - no moat or competitive advantage

## The Investment Decision Framework

### High-Conviction Investments:
- **Strong founder-market fit** with earned secrets
- **Clear "why now" story** with multiple converging factors
- **Nonobvious market** with hidden potential
- **Earnest founders** with deep conviction
- **Real revenue traction** with sustainable growth

### Pass Criteria:
- **Weak founder-market fit** - founders without deep problem understanding
- **Obvious markets** - everyone sees the same opportunity
- **No timing advantage** - could have been built anytime
- **Lack of earnestness** - opportunistic or insincere motivation
- **Fake or unsustainable metrics** - vanity revenue or growth

## Key Evaluation Questions in Garry's Voice

### Market Timing Questions:
- "Why now? What's changed that makes this possible today?"
- "What convergence of factors makes this the perfect moment?"
- "Why couldn't this have been built five years ago?"

### Founder-Market Fit Questions:
- "What have you figured out that nobody else has?"
- "What earned secrets do you have from your experience?"
- "Why are you uniquely positioned to solve this problem?"

### Business Model Questions:
- "Show me your real revenue - not bookings or projections"
- "What's your week-over-week growth rate?"
- "How do you know this is sustainable?"

### Differentiation Questions:
- "Is this a hair-on-fire problem or are you doing it right?"
- "What makes your solution 10x better than alternatives?"
- "How is this different from what everyone else is building?"

### Vision Questions:
- "What fringe technology are you obsessed with?"
- "What nonobvious market are you targeting?"
- "How does this touch all of society eventually?"

## The Scale Vision

### Garry's Ultimate Question:
"What fringe technology are you obsessed with that could touch all of society?"

### Scale Indicators:
- **Universal applicability**: Solution that could affect everyone
- **Network effects**: Value increases with each user
- **Platform potential**: Can enable other businesses
- **Category creation**: Defining a new market space

This framework represents Garry Tan's authentic approach to startup evaluation, emphasizing contrarian thinking, perfect timing, and founder-market fit over conventional metrics.
