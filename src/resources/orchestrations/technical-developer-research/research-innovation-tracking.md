# Research & Innovation Tracking Orchestration

This workflow discovers academic papers, patents, and research initiatives from a company's R&D teams, tracking their innovation efforts and predicting future product capabilities based on research investments.

## Overview

A company's research output and innovation activities provide early signals of future product directions and competitive advantages. This orchestration systematically tracks academic publications, patent filings, research collaborations, and innovation programs to understand technological capabilities and predict strategic developments.

## Required Tools

- `research_paper_search_exa` - Academic publication search
- `web_search_exa` - Patent and research lab information
- `linkedin_search_exa` - Research team composition
- `company_research_exa` - R&D investment context
- `github_search_exa` - Open research projects

## Best Practices

1. **Multi-Source Validation**: Cross-reference papers, patents, and hiring
2. **Timeline Calibration**: Track historical research-to-product times
3. **Network Analysis**: Map collaboration patterns for insights
4. **Quality Over Quantity**: Focus on high-impact research
5. **Competitive Context**: Always benchmark against peers

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

1. **Publication Bias**: Not all innovation is published
2. **Patent Interpretation**: Claims matter more than titles
3. **Time Lag**: Research precedes products by years
4. **Attribution Errors**: Verify researcher affiliations
5. **Over-extrapolation**: Basic research ≠ product guarantee
