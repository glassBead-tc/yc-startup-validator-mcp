# Retrieval Orchestration Use Cases with Exa MCP Tools

## Overview
The Exa MCP Server provides a suite of specialized search and data extraction tools (company info, competitor finder, web search, crawling, LinkedIn, YouTube, Reddit, TikTok, Wikipedia, GitHub, research papers, etc.) that can be orchestrated by an AI agent to perform complex information-gathering tasks. Below are several categories of retrieval orchestration use cases, each with multiple ideas (around 50 in total). These illustrate how an agent can combine multiple tools intelligently to fulfill various research and analysis goals.

## Business & Market Intelligence
- Comprehensive Company Profile: Orchestrate multiple tools (e.g. company_research_exa, wikipedia_search_exa, linkedin_search_exa) to gather a full profile of a company including history, leadership, products, financials, and culture, synthesized into an in-depth report.
- Industry Trend Report: Use broad web searches and Wikipedia (e.g. web_search_exa for news, wikipedia_search_exa for background) to collect data on market trends in an industry, then summarize key drivers, emerging technologies, and market projections.
- Market Overview & Sizing: Retrieve market research data by searching news, industry reports, and company filings (via web_search_exa and targeted site crawling) to estimate market size, growth rates, and major players in a sector.
- Product Launch Analysis: When a new product is launched, gather press releases, news coverage, and initial customer reactions (using web_search_exa for news and reddit_search_exa for user discussions) to evaluate the launch impact and public reception.
- Consumer Feedback Analysis: Aggregate customer opinions from forums and reviews (e.g. searching product name on Reddit via reddit_search_exa and relevant communities) to identify common praises and pain points for a company’s product or service.
- Executive Background Research: Compile information on key executives by searching professional profiles and news mentions (using linkedin_search_exa for career info and web_search_exa for interviews or news) to understand leadership experience and reputation.
- Investment & Financial Insights: Use web search and company info tools to find recent financial reports, stock performance news, or investor presentations, then summarize the company’s financial health, revenue growth, and investment highlights.
- News Coverage Summaries: Continuously monitor a company in the news (web_search_exa with date filters for recent articles) and produce a weekly summary of major announcements, controversies, or achievements affecting the company’s market position.
- Brand Reputation Audit: Combine social media and web data (Reddit discussions, news articles, possibly TikTok/YouTube mentions) to gauge public sentiment and brand reputation, highlighting positive buzz versus prevalent criticisms in the market.
- Regulatory Impact Assessment: Research any regulations, legal issues, or policy changes affecting the business (using web_search_exa for government or legal news and industry sites) and compile an analysis of how these external factors might influence the company or industry.

## Competitive Analysis & Strategy
- Competitor Identification & Profiling: Utilize the competitor finder tool (e.g. competitor_finder_exa) to list a company’s top competitors, then use company_research_exa and web searches to build profiles for each rival (covering their products, market share, strengths, and weaknesses).
- SWOT Analysis Aggregation: Automatically perform a SWOT analysis by retrieving information on competitors’ Strengths/Weaknesses (from company profiles, news, customer reviews) and Opportunities/Threats (from industry trend data, emerging market changes), synthesizing a comparative SWOT for strategic planning.
- Product Feature & Pricing Comparison: Gather data on competitors’ product features and pricing (via official websites using crawling_exa or web search) and compare them side-by-side with the target company’s offerings to identify competitive advantages or gaps.
- Content Marketing Benchmark: Compare the content strategies of multiple companies by scraping and analyzing their blogs, press releases, or YouTube channels (web_search_exa for blog posts, youtube_search_exa for videos). Summarize differences in messaging, audience engagement, and content frequency.
- Social Media Footprint Comparison: Use LinkedIn, YouTube, TikTok, and Reddit tools to evaluate each competitor’s presence and engagement on social platforms. This could include comparing follower counts, post frequency, video views, or community sentiment side-by-side.
- Market Share & Growth Insights: Retrieve any available market share statistics or growth metrics from business reports and news articles (web_search_exa targeting industry reports or analyst commentary) for each competitor. Compile these to see who is leading or lagging in the market and by how much.
- Customer Review Sentiment: Collect user reviews and discussions about competitors’ products (via Reddit or web forums search) to gauge customer satisfaction relative to the target company. The agent can then present a sentiment comparison (e.g. common complaints or praises for each brand).
- Partnerships and Alliances Research: Search news and press releases for announcements of partnerships, alliances, or major clients for each competitor. Summarize how each company leverages external relationships and how that strengthens their market position.
- Innovation & IP Scan: Use tools like research_paper_search_exa and github_search_exa to see if competitors are involved in R&D or open source. For example, find research papers published by or about them, or notable open-source projects they maintain, as an indicator of innovation.
- Digital Presence Audit: Examine each competitor’s digital footprint by crawling their website for SEO keywords, checking their online ad presence or traffic (via web search for any available analytics), and reviewing their engagement in online communities. This helps identify whose digital strategy is most robust and where others may be lacking.

## Social Media & Community Insights
- Cross-Platform Social Audit: Orchestrate a survey of the company’s presence across LinkedIn, YouTube, TikTok, and Reddit (linkedin_search_exa, youtube_search_exa, tiktok_search_exa, reddit_search_exa). The agent compiles metrics like follower counts, recent posts, video views, and community size to provide an overall social media health report.
- Sentiment Analysis Across Platforms: Collect and summarize audience sentiment by extracting comments or discussions from YouTube (via youtube_video_details_exa for comments), Reddit threads, and TikTok comments. The agent can identify common positive themes and frequent complaints or negative perceptions surrounding the brand.
- Influencer Identification: Search YouTube and TikTok for the most popular content related to the company or product (youtube_search_exa and tiktok_search_exa with the brand name). From the results, identify key influencers or channels driving the conversation, and have the agent gather stats on their reach and typical content tone.
- Viral Content Monitoring: Regularly query social platforms for any viral posts, memes, or trending videos involving the company or industry. For instance, use TikTok search to find if a challenge or trend is associated with the brand, and use Reddit search to see if any post about the company has gone viral, then summarize their impact.
- Reddit FAQ and Issue Analysis: Use reddit_search_exa to find frequently asked questions or common issues users discuss about the company’s product (e.g. search for “[Product Name] help” or “[Company] problem”). Aggregate the top recurring questions/complaints and the solutions or answers provided by the community.
- YouTube Content Trends: Analyze YouTube search results and video details for patterns in content about the company or product. For example, determine if most videos are reviews, tutorials, or commentary, and note view counts and like/dislike ratios to understand what content resonates with viewers.
- TikTok Trend Analysis: If the brand or its product appears in TikTok content, analyze the nature of these short videos (humorous takes, how-to use the product, etc.) and the engagement levels. This can reveal how younger audiences or consumers on TikTok perceive and interact with the brand.
- Social Engagement Benchmarking: Compare how the company’s social engagement (comments, shares, likes) stacks up against competitors. The agent can use platform-specific searches to fetch recent posts from each company and tally engagement metrics, highlighting which brand has more active or responsive followers.
- Crisis Monitoring on Social: In the event of a PR crisis or negative news, set the agent to monitor social channels for spikes in discussion. For example, track a Reddit thread or a viral tweet (via web search if needed) and compile the evolving sentiments and key points of discussion as the situation unfolds.
- Campaign Impact Evaluation: After a marketing campaign or event, use YouTube, TikTok, and Reddit searches to see how much buzz was generated. The agent can look for specific campaign hashtags or content, measure the volume of posts and views, and summarize audience reactions to gauge the campaign’s success.

## Technical & Developer Research
- Open Source Footprint Analysis: Utilize github_search_exa to find open-source repositories affiliated with the company (by organization or mentions). The agent can retrieve repository stats (stars, forks) and descriptions to summarize the company’s open-source contributions and their popularity in the developer community.
- Developer Community Feedback: Search developer forums and Reddit (e.g. subreddits like r/programming or technology-specific communities via reddit_search_exa) for discussions about the company’s developer tools or APIs. Summarize common praises, pain points, or wishes from developers interacting with the company’s technology.
- API Documentation Review: If the company offers APIs or SDKs, use crawling_exa on their documentation site to extract key information (endpoints, usage limits, examples). The agent can then present a condensed overview of the API capabilities and any developer comments about it (perhaps found via web search or Reddit).
- Tech Stack Discovery: Infer the company’s technology stack by combining clues from various sources: search LinkedIn for engineering job postings or employee profiles (to see listed skills), use web search for any tech blogs or case studies about their systems, and scan GitHub for any public code. This orchestrated info can reveal programming languages, cloud providers, or frameworks the company uses.
- Troubleshooting Knowledge Base: For a technical issue or error message related to the company’s product, the agent can perform a web search (stack overflow, GitHub issues, Reddit threads) to gather solutions. It then compiles a step-by-step troubleshooting guide or FAQ from the best answers found across sources.
- Framework/Tool Comparison: When deciding between two technologies (e.g. the company’s library vs a competitor’s), use github_search_exa to check adoption (number of contributors, activity) and reddit_search_exa to find developer opinions on each. The agent can then produce a comparison highlighting pros, cons, and use case fit for each option.
- Security Vulnerability Research: Query web search and research databases for any known security advisories, CVEs, or breach reports involving the company’s software or services. Summarize what vulnerabilities have been publicized and how the company responded, giving a picture of their security track record.
- Developer Hiring Trends: Leverage linkedin_search_exa to find recent job postings or hiring sprees in the company’s engineering departments. By noting what roles and skills are being hired, the agent can infer strategic areas of development (for example, many openings in AI or mobile suggest focus in those areas).
- Technical Feature Benchmarking: If comparing technical features (like performance, scalability, or compliance) of the company’s product versus competitors, have the agent gather benchmarks or case studies from web searches and technical whitepapers. It can then outline where each product stands out or lags behind on technical merits.
- Research & Innovation Tracking: Use research_paper_search_exa to find academic papers or patents authored by the company’s R&D teams or in partnership with universities. Also search news for any mentions of research labs or innovation programs. The agent compiles these to show the company’s involvement in cutting-edge research and how it might translate to future products.

## Knowledge & Academic Research
- Topic Literature Review: For a given scientific or technical topic, use research_paper_search_exa to pull relevant academic papers (titles, abstracts) and wikipedia_search_exa for background. The agent then synthesizes a coherent literature review, highlighting key findings, debates, and gaps in the research.
- Historical Event Timeline: Reconstruct a timeline of events for a significant occurrence (e.g. the evolution of a technology or a company’s history) by retrieving dates and facts from Wikipedia, news archives, and authoritative websites. The output is an ordered timeline with citations to each source.
- Fact-Checking Workflow: When given a factual claim or statistic, the agent can verify it by searching Wikipedia and reliable web sources. It will cross-reference multiple sources (web_search_exa for news or databases, plus Wikipedia) and report whether the claim holds true, along with evidence or corrected information if the claim is false or misleading.
- Educational Resource Aggregation: Compile a learning guide on a topic by finding high-quality tutorials, videos, and documentation across the web. For example, search YouTube for tutorial videos, use web search for beginner guides or official docs, and organize the findings into a structured curriculum or resource list for self-study.
- Comprehensive Q&A Resolution: For complex questions that require multi-step reasoning, the agent can perform a series of focused searches. For instance, to answer “How can quantum computing improve AI?”, it might gather background from Wikipedia on quantum computing, find research papers on quantum algorithms for AI, and search forums for expert discussions, then synthesize a well-rounded answer with all these facets.
- Domain Glossary Creation: Build a glossary of key terms in a specific domain (finance, medicine, etc.) by pulling definitions and explanations from sources like Wikipedia, academic sites, and industry websites. The agent ensures each term is clearly defined in plain language and provides context if necessary.
- Recent Research Trend Analysis: Use research_paper_search_exa to find the latest papers (e.g. in the past year) on an emerging technology or scientific question. Summarize common themes and breakthroughs, giving an overview of where the research frontier currently stands and what developments to watch for.
- Cross-Disciplinary Insight Gathering: For a multifaceted issue (like climate change impacts on economics), instruct the agent to retrieve information from different domains – scientific research, economic reports, social studies – using appropriate tools (academic search, web search, etc.). The outcome is a synthesis that combines perspectives from multiple fields.
- Public Figure or Organization Dossier: Compile a research dossier on a notable person or institution (using wikipedia_search_exa for basic info, web_search_exa for recent news, and linkedin_search_exa for professional background if applicable). The agent can gather biography details, notable achievements, controversies, and relevant statistics or facts, organizing them into a cohesive profile.
- Policy & Regulation Brief: When needing to understand a policy issue or new regulation, have the agent gather the text of the law (if available online), analyses from think tanks or news (web_search_exa for commentary), and related statistics. It can then produce a brief explaining the policy’s content, its intended effects, and various viewpoints or implications derived from the collected sources.

## Conclusion
Each of these ideas demonstrates how an AI agent with access to Exa's MCP server tools can intelligently combine searches and data extraction across multiple sources. By leveraging these orchestrated retrieval workflows, independent developers and analysts can obtain rich, up-to-date information and insights for a wide range of practical and creative applications. Each use case can be further expanded into a detailed workflow as needed, specifying the sequence of tool calls and the synthesis of results.
